## 4. Methodology

1. **Clean & explore** both datasets independently (shape, nulls, duplicates).
2. **Standardize dates** — `Timestamp IST` and Fear & Greed `date` are both parsed to a common `Date` column (date-only, no time) so they can be joined.
3. **Merge** trade-level data with the daily sentiment classification and numeric score via a left join on `Date`, preserving every trade.
4. **Engineer metrics**: win flag, per-trader win rate, per-trader average trade size, daily trade counts, long/short split, and fees — each broken out by sentiment regime.
5. **Segment traders** three ways: trade frequency (median split), win consistency (≥50% win rate), and position size (median split) — then compare total Closed PnL across each segment.
6. **Model**: a Random Forest classifier predicts whether an individual trade will be profitable, using sentiment score, trade size, execution price, fee, side, direction, and coin (one-hot encoded) as features. Evaluated with an 80/20 stratified train/test split.

## 5. Key Insights

**1. Extreme Greed and Fear — not calm Neutral days — produce the best trader outcomes.**
Average PnL and win rate are highest during Extreme Greed (\$67.89 avg PnL, 46.5% win rate), with Fear a close second (\$54.29, 42.1%) despite its cautious label. Neutral and Extreme Fear trail on both measures (~\$34 avg PnL). Median PnL is \$0 in every regime — most individual trades close near breakeven, and average profitability is driven by a smaller share of larger winning trades, not a shift in the typical trade.

**2. Traders take larger, more expensive positions specifically when the market is fearful.**
Fear has both the highest trade count (61,837) and the largest average trade size (\$7,816), alongside the highest average fee (\$1.50). Extreme Greed, by contrast, sees smaller average trades (\$3,112) and the lowest fees (\$0.68). Traders appear to size up during fear and pull back on individual bet size once the market is already euphoric.

**3. Win rate is a weak proxy for profitability — trading volume and risk/reward matter more.**
Segmenting traders shows Frequent Traders (\$496,528 total PnL) and High-Position-Size Traders (\$416,806) far outearn their counterparts — largely a volume effect. More strikingly, traders labeled "Inconsistent" (win rate < 50%, \$333,668 total PnL) outearn "Consistent Winners" (win rate ≥ 50%, \$206,867). A trader who wins less often but lets winning trades run can out-earn a high-win-rate trader who cuts winners short.

**4. Sell-side positioning increases as sentiment turns greedy.**
Extreme Greed shows the widest tilt toward SELL (22,052 vs. 17,940 BUY), while Extreme Fear is nearly balanced (10,935 BUY vs. 10,465 SELL) — consistent with traders taking profit or shorting into strength, and staying closer to neutral during fear.

**5. (Bonus model) Trade structure predicts profitability better than sentiment does.**
The Random Forest model reaches ~98.3% test accuracy (precision 0.97, recall 0.99, F1 0.98). The most important features are all trade-direction flags and execution price; the Fear & Greed `value` ranks 7th of 17 features. *Caveat: `Direction` values like Close Long/Close Short mark the closing leg of a position and may correlate mechanically with the sign of Closed PnL — this is a plausible source of leakage, and the reported accuracy should be re-checked with `Direction` removed before treating it as fully generalizable.*

## 6. Strategy Recommendations (Actionable Output)

**Strategy 1 — Cap position size on Fear days for smaller/infrequent traders.**
Fear is the regime with the largest average trade size and highest fees, but it is *not* the regime with the best win rate or average PnL — that's Extreme Greed. For traders in the "Infrequent" or "Low Position Size" segments, increasing size specifically on Fear days adds fee drag without a corresponding edge. Rule of thumb: hold position size at Neutral-day levels (~\$4,800 avg) during Fear regimes unless already in the "High Position Size" segment.

**Strategy 2 — Let winners run rather than optimizing for win rate.**
Because "Inconsistent" traders (win rate < 50%) generate more total PnL than "Consistent Winners," chasing a higher win rate is not necessarily the right lever. Rule of thumb: for traders already in the "Frequent" or "High Position Size" segments, prioritize favorable risk/reward per trade (e.g., wider profit targets, tighter stops) over win-rate optimization — especially during Extreme Greed, where the data shows the best historical average outcomes.

## 7. Limitations & Next Steps

- Only 479 of the ~730 days in the trade history overlap with sentiment data; 6 trades remain unmatched after the merge.
- The profitability model's accuracy may be inflated by leakage from the `Direction` feature (see caveat above) — re-running without it would give a cleaner read on how much sentiment and price alone can predict profitability.
- Trader segmentation uses single-metric median splits; a multi-feature clustering approach (e.g., k-means on frequency, size, and win rate together) could reveal more nuanced behavioral archetypes.
